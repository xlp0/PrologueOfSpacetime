### How It Works: The Tunnel Algorithm

The sundial contains hundreds of precisely calculated internal tunnels. As the sun moves across the sky, different combinations of these tunnels align to allow light to pass through, projecting digital numbers onto a surface.

*   **Input**: The sun's position (azimuth and elevation angles).
*   **Process**: Light rays travel through specific tunnel combinations based on the sun's angle.
*   **Output**: A digital time display.

This is **analog computation**â€”the physical structure itself is the algorithm. The geometry of the tunnels encodes the function:

$$\text{SunPosition}(\theta, \phi, \text{latitude}, \text{date}) \to \text{DigitalTime}$$

Where:

- The tunnels act as a **lookup table** carved into 3D space

## 3. Technical Implementation: 3D Printing the Cosmos

The Digital Sundial is one of the most elegant demonstrations of **additive manufacturing**â€”building complexity layer by layer.

### Material Selection: ABS vs. PLA

*   **ABS (Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene)**: Recommended. Heat-resistant (glass transition ~105Â°C), durable in direct sunlight. Will not deform on a hot summer day.
*   **PLA (Polylactic Acid)**: Not recommended. Lower heat resistance (~60Â°C), prone to warping and softening in direct sun exposure.

The choice of material is a lesson in **thermodynamics**: the sundial must survive the very heat source (the sun) that powers it.

### The Print Parameters

*   **Layer Height**: 0.2mm or finer. The tunnels require precisionâ€”coarse layers will block light paths.
*   **Infill**: 100%. The internal structure must be solid to create clean light tunnels. Any voids will cause light leakage and blur the digits.
*   **Support Structures**: Required for the overhanging tunnels. These must be carefully removed post-print without damaging the delicate internal geometry.
*   **Print Time**: 12-24 hours depending on size. This is a **patience exercise**â€”rushing the print will ruin the optical precision.

### Post-Processing: The Reveal

After printing, the sundial requires careful cleanup:
1. **Support Removal**: Use needle-nose pliers and patience. Each tunnel must be clear.
2. **Surface Finishing**: Light sanding of the base to ensure it sits flat.
3. **Testing**: Take it outside at noon (solar noon, not clock noon) to verify the digits appear correctly.
